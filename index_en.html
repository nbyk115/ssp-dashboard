Japan ads.txt SSP Presence Analysis

Analyzing ads.txt entries from major Japanese publishers to visualize supply path structure and scale of key SSPs in the Japan market

ads.txt collected from 115 corporate media sites filtered from top 200 by Potential Impressions14 SSPs analyzed (excl. Google AdX, APS, Criteo, Outbrain/Taboola, Hakuhodo DY group, FreakOut)2026-02-18 crawl / imp 2/10–16

Data Sources & Exclusions

📊 Data Source
  • Japan sites ranked by Potential Impressions (est. pageviews), top 200 sites
  • Filtered to 115 corporate-operated editorial media sites for ads.txt collection
  • Primarily newspapers, TV networks, publisher web media, and major portals
  • Impression period: Feb 10–16, 2026
🔍 Site Filtering Criteria
  • No industry-standard definition of "premium" exists
  • For SSP evaluation, inventory must be corporate-operated × editorial content × brand-safe
  • UGC/personal blogs, game wikis, suspected MFA sites, and tool sites excluded
  • Including these would inflate reseller paths and distort SSP evaluation
🚫 SSP Exclusions (5)
  • Criteo — Own DSP (Commerce Grid) demand dominates. Limited benefit buying through Criteo via TTD/DV360
    Ref: 1,554 paths / D-rate 48% / 110 sites
  • Outbrain, Taboola — Native ad networks. Closed recommendation demand structure; not comparable with open auction SSPs
    Ref: Outbrain 890 paths/D-rate 36%, Taboola 335 paths/D-rate 84%
  • YieldOne(impact-ad.jp)— Hakuhodo DY group SSP. Structurally high D-rate due to in-group media connections. Not recommended from Dentsu group perspective
    Ref: 2,143 paths / D-rate 20% / 112 sites
📋 About ADNW / HB Wrappers
  • ADNW (Ad Network) = Network bundling ad inventory from multiple media for sale. Unlike SSPs, not typically subject to individual path selection (SPO) from DSPs
  • HB Wrapper = Platform managing connections to multiple SSPs on the publisher side (Header Bidding Wrapper). Not directly buyable from DSPs
  • This analysis evaluates SSPs from a DSP perspective; ADNW-type (AdStir, Ad Generation, i-mobile, AJA SSP, etc.) and HB wrapper specialists (FLUX, FourM, etc.) are excluded
  • Note: Magnite, fluct, and Geniee also operate HB wrapper businesses but are classified as SSPs since that is their primary function
⚠️ Other Exclusions
  • Google AdX(google.com)— 10,311 paths / all 115 sites. Incomparable scale; reported separately
  • Amazon APS(TAM/UAM)— Excluded due to structural advantage from Amazon DSP demand
  • FreakOut(fout.jp)— DSP specialist. Minimal SSP function (12 paths)
* adingo.jp is the ads.txt domain for fluct (CARTA HD / Dentsu Group). Not DAC.
"Path count" means: The number of authorization lines in each site's ads.txt (ad sales authorization list) that say "OK to sell ads via this SSP." Each site has multiple accounts/paths, so totals across 115 sites reach thousands to tens of thousands. More paths = more sites authorizing that SSP through more routes. This is not request count or impression count.
